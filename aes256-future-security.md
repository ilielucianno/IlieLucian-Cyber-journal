# 🔐 AES-256 Security in the Age of Exponential Computing Growth

**Author**: Lucian  
**Date**: March 2026  
**Context**: Deep discussion about whether AES-256 will remain secure given accelerating computing power

## 🤔 The Question

> "Can someone rent massive computing power (cloud computing) to brute-force AES-256 encryption?"

## 💡 My Analysis & Observations

### The Classic Calculation

According to cybersecurity research:
- Breaking AES-256 requires 2²⁵⁵ attempts (50% chance)
- Even at 1 electronvolt per attempt, you'd need **9.26 × 10⁵⁷ Joules**
- That's equivalent to **10,000 Jupiter-sized planets** converted to pure energy
- With all Earth's computing power combined: **145 million years**

### The Cloud Computing Reality

| Resource | Cost | Speed |
|----------|------|-------|
| NVIDIA V100 GPU | $4/hour | ~195 billion keys/sec |
| 1 million GPUs | $4M/hour | Still 145M years for AES-256 |

### 🧠 My Critical Observation

**But here's what the calculations miss:**

I've been using computers since 1995 (HC, HC2000, Windows 95, QBasic, Turbo Pascal). I've witnessed firsthand:

| Period | Computing Growth |
|--------|-----------------|
| 1995-2000 | 100x (slow, predictable) |
| 2020-2025 | 100x (exponential acceleration) |

The growth rate in the last 5 years is **qualitatively different** from 1995-2000. Specialized hardware (GPUs, TPUs), cloud computing, and AI accelerators are changing the game.

### 🔮 What This Means for AES-256

If growth continues accelerating, the timeline for breaking AES-256 shrinks dramatically — which is exactly why cryptographers are already working on **post-quantum cryptography**.
